KTU 2024 Scheme PECST419 Cyber Ethics & Privacy

Module 1 – Cyber Law & Cyberspace

Q1. What is Cyber Law? What are the major areas covered under Cyber Law?

Answer:

Cyber Law is the legal framework governing computers, internet usage, and digital communication. It regulates online activities and protects users from cybercrimes. Major areas include cybercrime, data protection, privacy, intellectual property rights, e-commerce, digital signatures, and cybersecurity.

Q2. What are the major objectives of Cyber Law?

Answer:

Objectives of Cyber Law include preventing cybercrime, protecting privacy and personal data, regulating online activities, promoting secure digital transactions, and ensuring a safe cyberspace for individuals and organizations.

Module 2 – Cyber Crimes & Cyber Ethics

Q1. What is cybercrime? Explain the main categories of cybercrime.

Answer:

Cybercrime refers to unlawful activities committed using computers or the internet. Major categories are crimes against individuals, property, organizations, and society.

Module 3 – Privacy & Data Protection

Q1. Why is data protection important in cyberspace?

Answer:

Data protection safeguards sensitive information from unauthorized access, misuse, loss, theft, and cyberattacks. It ensures privacy, trust, and compliance with legal standards.

Module 4 – IT Act & Information Security

Q1. Explain the need for an Information Security Policy.

Answer:

An Information Security Policy defines rules and procedures to protect organizational data, systems, and digital resources from cyber threats and unauthorized access.
